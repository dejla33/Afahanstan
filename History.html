History of Afghanistan

Ghaznavid Empire

The Ghaznavid Empire (977–1186), founded by Sultan Mahmud of Ghazni, was a Persianate Muslim dynasty that ruled parts of Persia, Central Asia, and the Indian subcontinent. Ghazni became a center of learning and culture.

Ghurid Empire

The Ghurids (1149–1215) emerged from the Ghor region of Afghanistan. They expanded into northern India, helping spread Islamic culture and architecture across the region.

Timurid Empire

The Timurid Empire (1370–1507) was founded by Timur (Tamerlane). It was known for its cultural achievements, especially in Herat, which became a hub of art, science, and Persian literature.

Durrani Empire

Founded in 1747 by Ahmad Shah Durrani, the Durrani Empire is seen as the foundation of modern Afghanistan. It united many Afghan tribes and established Afghanistan as an independent state.
